Other Permanent Housing (OPH)

Provides long-term housing that is not classified as Permanent Supportive Housing or Rapid Re-housing, aimed at offering stable housing solutions.

Qualification
Homeless

Requires individuals or families experiencing homelessness.

Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH)

Long-term housing with supportive services aimed at homeless individuals with disabilities. Chronic homelessness and a documented disabling condition are required for entry.

Locations: Available at many sites within Housing Hope.

Qualification
Chronically Homeless

Requires chronic homelessness and a disabling condition.

Mental Health Permanent Supportive Housing (MH PSH)

A subset of PSH providing housing and mental health services, focusing on individuals with significant mental health issues.

Qualification
Chronically Homeless

Requires chronic homelessness and significant mental health challenges.

Chemical Dependency Mental Health (CDMH)

CDMH-designated units are housing units specifically set aside for individuals experiencing both substance use disorders and mental health issues. These units are often part of Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH) programs, providing stable housing combined with supportive services to address the complex needs of this population.

Goals of the CDMH Program

The primary goal of housing individuals in CDMH units is to offer stability through a Housing First approach, meaning housing is provided without preconditions such as sobriety or treatment compliance. Once housed, individuals can better engage in supportive services aimed at recovery and overall health. Housing reduces the stress and instability that exacerbate mental health and substance use issues. CDMH programs aim to improve quality of life, reduce homelessness, and lower public costs related to emergency services, incarceration, and hospitalizations.

Why House CDMH Individuals?

Housing people experiencing mental health and chemical dependency helps stabilize their living situation, which is often a key barrier to effective treatment. When individuals have a safe place to live, they are more likely to access and benefit from services such as counseling, substance use treatment, and case management. Stable housing reduces the risk of relapse and the negative impacts of homelessness, including physical health deterioration and increased interactions with law enforcement.

Qualification
Chronically Homeless

Requires chronic homelessness and severe mental health or substance use disorders that significantly impair the ability to live independently. Entry assessments may be required to confirm the need for both housing and treatment services.

Emergency Shelter

Provides temporary shelter for up to 53 days, offering immediate relief and basic necessities to homeless individuals and families.

Locations: A house in Stanwood and apartment-style shelter units in Everett.

Qualification
Homeless

Open to individuals and families experiencing homelessness.

Teen and Young Parent

Focuses on helping teen and young parents aged 16-24 and their children who are enrolled in the Teen and Young Parent program.

Qualification
Homeless

For teen and young parents experiencing homelessness.

CoC/Station Place Beachwood CoC

Permanent CoC units function similarly to PSH but differ in specific target populations, services provided, and funding structures. A few units designated under the CoC program were moved to Station Place but retain their CoC designation.

Qualification
Chronically Homeless

Requires chronic homelessness with specific program criteria.

Families with Children

Houses families specifically, sometimes accommodating newborns, emphasizing a stable environment conducive to family growth.

Qualification
Homeless

Designed for families experiencing homelessness.

Veterans (VASH - Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing)

Combines HUD housing vouchers with VA supportive services to help veterans who are homeless. Referrals typically come from the VA, and an additional VA staff member helps with case management.

Qualification
Homeless

Exclusive to veterans experiencing homelessness.

Young Adults

Houses young adults, aiming to intervene early and provide stability to prevent long-term homelessness.

Qualification
Homeless

For young adults experiencing homelessness.
