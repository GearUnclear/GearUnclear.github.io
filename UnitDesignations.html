Other Permanent Housing (OPH)

Provides long-term housing that is not classified as Permanent Supportive Housing or Rapid Re-housing, aimed at offering stable housing solutions.

Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH)

Long-term housing with supportive services aimed at homeless individuals with disabilities. Chronic homelessness and a documented disabling condition are required for entry.

Locations: Available at many sites within Housing Hope.

Mental Health Permanent Supportive Housing (MH PSH)

A subset of PSH providing housing and mental health services, focusing on individuals with significant mental health issues.

Emergency Shelter

Provides temporary shelter for up to 53 days, offering immediate relief and basic necessities to homeless individuals and families.

Locations: A house in Stanwood and apartment-style shelter units in Everett.

Teen and Young Parent

Focuses on helping teen and young parents aged 16-24 and their children who are enrolled in the Teen and Young Parent program.

CoC/Station Place Beachwood CoC

Permanent CoC units would function similarly to PSH in that they aim to provide stable, long-term housing to help individuals and families achieve and maintain housing stability. However, they might differ in terms of the specific target populations, the types of services provided, and the funding structures. A few units designated under the CoC program were moved to Station Place but retain their CoC designation, and their differentiated funding layers.

Families with Children

Houses families specifically, sometimes accommodating incoming newborns, emphasizing a stable environment conducive to family growth.

Veterans (VASH - Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing)

Combines HUD housing vouchers with VA supportive services to help veterans who are homeless. Referrals typically come from the VA, and an additional VA staff member helps with case management.

Young Adults

Houses young adults, aiming to intervene early and provide stability to prevent long-term homelessness.
