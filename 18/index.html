18-Year-Old Assessment Requirements

Required assessment procedures when minor children reach age 18

1 When a Minor Child Turns 18

When a minor child in Housing Hope housing reaches age 18, HUD requires an update assessment to document fields that are not completed for children or may have changed upon reaching adulthood. This assessment captures information that becomes relevant or required once the individual is legally an adult.

18-Year-Old Update Assessment

Upon turning 18, an update assessment must be completed to document adult-specific information. This includes:

  • Fields that are not assessed for minor children but become required for adults
  • Any income that was previously recorded under the head of household must now be documented under this individual
  • Adult-specific assessment areas that may have changed since becoming legally independent

Critical: The 'Date of Assessment' must match the literal 18th birthday of the client to avoid data errors.

CRITICAL: The following three fields are MANDATORY and must be completed when creating an adult assessment for someone who has just turned 18. Failure to complete these fields will result in incomplete HMIS data submission.

Mandatory Fields - Must Complete All Three

  • Domestic Violence Assessment: Must be completed to ensure proper anonymization protocols in the HMIS database. This field is critical for client safety and data security.
  • Financial/Income Assessment: All income sources, employment status, and financial information must be documented as the individual is now an independent adult client.
  • Health Insurance Coverage: Health insurance status and coverage information must be assessed and documented for the new adult client.

3 Additional Assessment Areas

Beyond the three mandatory fields above, the following areas should also be assessed for the new adult client:

  • Non-Cash Benefits: Document any government assistance or benefits
  • Employment Status: Current employment situation and work history
  • Disability Status: Any physical, developmental, or chronic health conditions
  • Mental Health: Mental health status and any current treatment
  • Substance Use: Assessment of substance use patterns
  • Fenn-Jorstad Self-Sufficiency Matrix: Comprehensive assessment measuring progress toward self-sufficiency across 25 outcome scales

Note: While these additional areas are important for comprehensive data collection, the three fields highlighted in Step 2 are the absolute mandatory requirements that must be completed.

4 Documentation and Follow-up

Once the 18-year-old update assessment is completed in Apricot:

  • The data team will be automatically notified of the completed assessment
  • Updated information will be processed and entered into the HMIS database
  • The individual's adult assessment data will be reflected in all Housing Hope systems
  • This completes the HUD-required update assessment for the age transition

Timeline: All mandatory fields must be completed in an update assessment dated Client Birthday.
