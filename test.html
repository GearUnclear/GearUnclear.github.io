HUD Assessment Guidelines

Move-in Assessment

There is no requirement to complete a new assessment upon move-in. However, HUD guidelines indicate the need for updating specific records during the project stay, when income or household changes occur.

Income and Sources

An Income and Sources record must be created during the project stay if there is any change in income or sources, at any time during the resident's stay at Housing Hope. This includes:

  • When a minor child enters or leaves the household, leading to a change in household income.
  • When a minor child turns 18, any income associated with the new adult client should be recorded.

If income shifts between household members, an additional record must be created to reflect this. 'Information Date' for these records should match the date of data collection.

Additional Required Records

According to HUD's Data Standards Manual for 2024, the following areas also require updates when changes occur:

  • Domestic Violence: Records should be updated to ensure swift anonymization in the database.
  • Non-Cash Benefits
  • Health Insurance

Recommended Updates

While not required, it is recommended to update the following records during the project stay:

  • Substance Use
  • Physical Disability
  • Developmental Disability
  • Chronic Health Condition
  • Mental Health Disorder

Streamlined Assessment Process

We will be updating the assessment form in Apricot to allow updates for specific elements, eliminating the need to complete the entire form when changes occur.

Summary

Must Update HUD upon any changes:

  • Income and Non-Cash Benefits
  • Domestic Violence
  • Health Insurance

Recommended to update HUD upon any changes:

  • Substance Use
  • Physical Disability
  • Developmental Disability
  • Chronic Health Condition
  • Mental Health Disorder
