HUD Housing Unit Designations Explained

1. Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH)

Description: Long-term housing with supportive services aimed at homeless individuals with disabilities. Chronic homelessness and a documented disabling condition are required for entry.

Locations: Available at many sites within our network.

Relevant Program: Continuum of Care (CoC) Program.

2. MH PSH (Mental Health Permanent Supportive Housing)

Description: A subset of PSH providing housing and mental health services. This program focuses on individuals with significant mental health issues.

Locations: Available at a few selected sites.

3. Emergency Shelter

Description: Provides temporary shelter for up to 53 days. Offers immediate relief and basic necessities to homeless individuals and families.

Locations: A house in Stanwood and apartment-style shelter units in Everett.

4. Teen and Young Parent

Description: Focuses on helping teen and young parents aged 16-24 and their children who are enrolled in the Teen and Young Parent program.

5. Station Place Beachwood CoC

Description: A few units designated under the CoC program were moved to Station Place but retain their CoC designation, focusing on integrated community support.

6. Families with Children

Description: Houses families specifically, sometimes accommodating incoming newborns, emphasizing a stable environment conducive to family growth.

7. Veterans (VASH - Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing)

Description: Combines HUD housing vouchers with VA supportive services to help veterans who are homeless. Referrals for these units typically come from the VA, and there is an additional VA staff member for case management.

8. Young Adults

Description: Houses young adults, aiming to intervene early and provide stability to prevent long-term homelessness.
