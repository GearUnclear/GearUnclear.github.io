Update Assessments, and Whole Family Move-in Guidelines

Family Move-in and update assessment procedures according to HUD standards

1 Move-in Assessment Overview

There is no requirement to complete a new assessment upon move-in. This page outlines what must be updated during a project stay to keep HUD data accurate and current. If any of the detailed changes below have occurred since the client's intake, an Update Assessment is required to record those changes. If nothing has changed, no move-in assessment is needed.

Use this guidance whenever a household composition, income, employment, or benefit status changes (including when a youth turns 18). These rules apply throughout the stay and ensure that the HMIS record remains complete and compliant with HUD standards.

2 Income and Sources (Required when changes occur)

Create or update an Income and Sources record during the project stay if there is any change in income or sources at any time during the resident's stay. This includes:

  • When a minor child enters or leaves the household, changing total household income.
  • When a minor turns 18: any income associated with the new adult client should be recorded (income prior to 18 should have been recorded under the head of household).
  • When income shifts between household members.

Set the Information Date to the date the information was collected. Whenever Income and Sources is updated, complete the Employment section as well so related information stays aligned.

3 Additional Required Records

According to HUD's Data Standards Manual for 2024, the following areas also require updates when changes occur:

  • Domestic Violence: Update promptly to ensure swift anonymization in the database.
  • Non-Cash Benefits
  • Health Insurance

4 Recommended Updates

While not required, it is recommended to update the following records during the project stay:

  • Substance Use
  • Physical Disability
  • Developmental Disability
  • Chronic Health Condition
  • Mental Health Disorder

5 Streamlined Assessment Process

The assessment form in Apricot has been updated to allow updates for specific elements, eliminating the need to complete the entire form when changes occur. When an update assessment is selected, a brief version of this guidance is presented, along with choices for which sections you would like to update. In some situations, such as a child becoming an adult, some sections may be required even if no changes occurred.

6 18th Birthday Assessment Requirements

When a child in the household turns 18, specific assessment sections are mandatory regardless of whether changes have occurred.

HUD requires updates for the following in this situation:
• Income and Non-Cash Benefits
• Domestic Violence
• Health Insurance
⚠️
Timeliness for Update Assessments

HMIS has indicated limited flexibility for 18th birthday update assessments; these do not need to be completed within three days of the birthday. That said, please enter the data as quickly as possible.

For all other updates (income, household composition, benefits, insurance, etc.), there is no change to expectations: complete updates within three days of becoming aware of the change.

Summary

Must Update HUD upon any changes:

  • Income and Non-Cash Benefits (including Employment)
  • Domestic Violence
  • Health Insurance

Recommended to update HUD upon any changes:

  • Substance Use
  • Physical Disability
  • Developmental Disability
  • Chronic Health Condition
  • Mental Health Disorder
