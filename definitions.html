Homelessness Definitions

HUD official definitions for literal and chronic homelessness classifications
1

Homeless

Definition:

According to HUD, an individual or family is considered homeless if they lack a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence. This broad category encompasses what is specifically defined as Literal Homelessness. It includes:

  • Unsheltered: Individuals living in places not designed for human habitation, such as streets, parks, vehicles, abandoned buildings, or other public spaces.
  • Sheltered: Individuals residing in emergency shelters, transitional housing programs, or other temporary accommodations provided by shelters.
  • Imminent Risk of Homelessness: Individuals who are at immediate risk of losing their current housing and becoming homeless (e.g., facing eviction or foreclosure).
  • Category 4 - DV Survivors: Survivors of domestic violence (DV) fleeing dangerous situations without safe alternative housing.
  • Literal Homelessness: Encompasses individuals and families without a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence.
  • Includes those living in shelters, on the streets, in vehicles, abandoned buildings, or other unsuitable locations.
  • DV survivors are recognized under this category when they flee dangerous environments without safe housing alternatives.
  • Recognizes the immediate needs and vulnerabilities of individuals in precarious living situations.
2

Chronically Homeless

Definition:

Chronically homeless individuals are those who meet the following criteria as defined by HUD:

  • Duration: Have been homeless continuously for at least one year or have experienced four or more episodes of homelessness in the past three years.
  • Disability: Possess a disability (physical or mental) that substantially impairs their ability to secure or maintain stable housing.
  • Chronic homelessness is characterized by long-term or repeated episodes of homelessness.
  • The presence of a disabling condition is a critical factor in this classification.
  • This designation helps in prioritizing services and support for those with the most persistent and complex housing needs.

Summary

  • Homeless: Individuals and families lacking a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence, including those in shelters, transitional housing, unsheltered environments, and DV survivors fleeing dangerous situations (Category 4).
  • Chronically Homeless: A subset of the homeless population with long-term or repeated homelessness coupled with a disabling condition that hinders their ability to maintain stable housing.

Understanding these definitions is crucial for policymakers, service providers, and communities to effectively address and support individuals experiencing homelessness.
