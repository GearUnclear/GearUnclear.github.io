Detailed Protocol & Reporting Information

Overview & Purpose

Events occur within Housing Hope and HopeWorks programs which require awareness at various levels of the organization and documentation. Timely reporting and documentation of these incidents allows the organization to remain responsive to dangerous situations or circumstances that place our operations at risk. Your support of this policy will help assure our collective safety and the well-being of residence, staff, and the community.

Emergency Medical Attention

Prior to reporting an incident to Housing Hope and HopeWorks, anyone experiencing a serious or life-threatening injury or illness must seek medical attention first:

  • Call 911, or
  • Seek medical care at the Emergency Room

Those Individuals experiencing a non-life-threatening issue should visit a Walk-In Clinic.

Incident Categories (The 5 P's)

Please make note of the time frames for submitting reports for incidents involving:

  • Personal injury (staff, resident, client, volunteer, visitor)
  • Property damage (theft, fire, building damage)
  • Police/fire/ambulance call
  • Public relations issues (litigation threatened, neighbor hostility, media investigations/inquiries, etc.)
  • Program/client related (rules violations, threats)

General Reporting Requirements

Incidents are to be documented by any staff member who witnesses the event. Where there was no staff present the staff member who learns of the incident provides written documentation. Include the following:

  • Date and time of the incident.
  • Location and/or address of the incident.
  • Description of what happened.
  • Who was involved and any witnesses. Include Names, Positions or Relation to Housing Hope.
  • Type and severity of harm or injury sustained.
  • Medical treatments administered to address the incident.
  • Steps taken to address the incident.
  • Attach any police reports, photos, videos, or documents (via email to frontdesk@housinghope.org).

Employee Incident Requirements

In addition to the general requirements, employee incidents must include:

  • Worker's name and contact information.
  • Details of the employee's duties when the incident occurred.
  • Description of what happened and type of concern.
  • Any near-miss conditions or safety violations that were present.
  • Corrective action taken to prevent similar events from happening in the future.

Notify department supervisor within 2 hours if not sooner.

Complete the Incident Report form within 24 hours.

Submission & Follow-up

All Incident Reports will be logged in and forwarded to the Housing Hope and HopeWorks Strategic Leadership Team and the Department Directors. The Department Director will route the report to all pertinent staff, secure input, and determine actions to be taken based on safety policies, insurance coverage, training needs, public relations responses and fiscal impacts.

Questions? Contact your Leadership Team or frontdesk@housinghope.org
